Warehouse Cycle Counter - Cilmore Shoprite DC

2019 - 2021

The role of a Warehouse Cycle Counter is critical in ensuring that inventory records are accurate, up-to-date, and reliable. As part of the cycle counting process, the Warehouse Cycle Counter is responsible for physically counting inventory in the warehouse, reconciling discrepancies, and supporting the overall inventory management system. Below are the key details about the roles and responsibilities:

Role of a Warehouse Cycle Counter

The primary role is to perform inventory counts at regular intervals to ensure that the physical stock matches the system records. Unlike full physical inventories done once or twice a year, cycle counts are ongoing and focus on small sections, helping to identify and resolve discrepancies early.

Key Responsibilities

  • Perform Inventory Counts: Carry out scheduled counts for specific areas/items, ensuring accuracy and reporting discrepancies.
  • Reconcile Discrepancies: Identify, report, and investigate differences between physical counts and system data, assisting in record adjustments.
  • Record Results: Log counts and discrepancies in the inventory system or forms, using barcode/RFID tools or manual entries.
  • Use Technology: Operate barcode scanners, RFID devices, and inventory software to facilitate accurate counts.
  • Verify Locations & Accessibility: Ensure items are properly labeled, organized, and accessible for efficient counting.
  • Collaborate: Work with supervisors, receiving, and shipping teams to ensure inventory accuracy and help in stock replenishment.
  • Follow Safety & Procedures: Use PPE, handle items safely, and follow safety guidelines.
  • Contribute to Improvements: Suggest process improvements for labeling, organization, and counting efficiency.

Tools & Skills

  • Barcode scanners, RFID systems
  • Inventory management software
  • Attention to detail and organization
  • Problem-solving and communication skills
  • Physical stamina for warehouse tasks

Challenges & Contributions

Ensuring accuracy amid repetitive tasks, physical demands, and reducing discrepancies, which supports inventory integrity and prevents stock issues.

Warehouse Operator - Twinnsaver

2021 - 2024

A Warehouse Operator plays a critical role in managing and moving inventory within a warehouse environment. Their responsibilities span tasks from receiving goods and organizing inventory to picking, packing, and shipping orders. They ensure that warehouse operations are efficient, inventory is properly handled, and orders are fulfilled on time.

Roles & Responsibilities

  • Receiving & Unloading: Unload goods from trucks, inspect for damages, verify against documentation, and label products.
  • Storage & Inventory Management: Organize stock in proper locations, monitor levels, and rotate stock (FIFO).
  • Order Picking & Packing: Retrieve items based on orders, pack securely, label, and prepare for dispatch.
  • Shipping & Dispatch: Load shipments, verify shipping details, coordinate with logistics.
  • Assist in Cycle Counts: Physically count stock, report discrepancies, and help update records.
  • Warehouse Maintenance: Keep work areas clean, organized, and safe; inspect equipment.
  • Operate Equipment: Use forklifts, pallet jacks, and other machinery safely and effectively.
  • Quality & Order Accuracy: Inspect products for defects, ensure correct orders, and minimize errors.
  • Teamwork & Communication: Collaborate with departments, report issues, and suggest process improvements.

Skills & Tools

  • Attention to detail
  • Physical strength and stamina
  • Basic computer skills & WMS familiarity
  • Forklift certification (if applicable)
  • Good communication & teamwork
  • Safety-consciousness

Challenges

Handling physical demands, maintaining accuracy under time pressure, and ensuring safety are key challenges faced in this role.
